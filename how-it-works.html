How it Works?

Recent advances in wireless sensors, alongside ubiquitous availability of mobile (GSM) networks in developing countries now make it possible to remotely and automatically monitor the quality of water being dispensed. Our proposed device, called waterATM:

  1. An automated water-dispensing unit
    • Uses RFID authentication for each family
    • Solenoid activated valve to start and stop dispensing of water
    • Flow meter used to measure volume of water dispensed
  2. A real-time remote water quality-monitoring unit
    • Measure turbidity, residual chlorine, total dissolved solids and pH
  3. Communications protocols
    • Two SIM GSP components (one for backup) where the log files from the water dispensing unit and water quality unit can be pushed to a central database in real time
    • A local database will be kept on the system to sore data in the case that neither GSM network is connecting
